Storyline

CarMax marketers launch lifecycle campaigns in MCA. They draft with Campaign Agent, build the audience using Einstein Segmentation, enrich and prioritize it with CIs, engagement data, and waterfall logic, then deliver web-engagement-triggered dynamic email content, orchestrate with SMS in MCA Flow, close with reporting, and finish with a concept-only dynamic feedback loop stop.

Keep this positioning line consistent: Data Cloud supports the decisioning layer; MCA is the execution center, with web engagement already stream-fed for activation.

Harbor Cruise Run of Show (60 min)

Dock: Business Framing
4 min
  • Set objective and KPI intent.
  • Frame the end-to-end live flow.
Stop 1: Campaign Agent
9 min
  • Draft campaign from business brief.
  • Validate message and channel direction.
Stop 2: Segmentation + CIs + Engagement + Waterfall
16 min
  • Create audience with Einstein natural language.
  • Refine rules with value/recency/loyalty fields.
  • Add CI and engagement overlays.
  • Demonstrate waterfall prioritization for overlap.
Stop 3: Web-Triggered Dynamic Email
10 min
  • Use "view a car page" as the trigger scenario via stream-fed web engagement data.
  • Show dynamic content blocks and repeaters/loops for personalized modules.
  • Preview multiple personas and outcomes.
Stop 4: MCA Flow + Upper Funnel SMS
9 min
  • Entry logic, branching, waits, and suppression paths.
  • Show SMS authoring in MCA and SMS inclusion in campaign flow.
  • Show controlled cross-channel execution.
Stop 5: Reporting & Optimization
5 min
  • Show campaign and journey outcomes.
  • Identify next optimization decision.
Stop 6: Dynamic Feedback Loop Concept
5 min
  • Present concept graphic for in-email feedback loops and recommendation refinement.
  • Clarify this step is explanatory and not live-demonstrated in this session.
Wrap: Executive Recap
2 min
  • Restate value: speed, precision, and control.
  • Close with adoption confidence.

Use Cases in the Demo

  • Primary: Web-engagement-triggered email (for example, "view a car page").
  • Feature moment: Dynamic content blocks + loop/repeater personalization in email.
  • Channel proof: Upper-funnel SMS authored in MCA and placed in flow.
  • Late concept moment: Dynamic feedback loops explained with visual narrative (no live demo).
  • Governance moment: Waterfall prioritization + engagement suppression.
  • Keep Stop 2 as one cohesive targeting/prioritization block.
  • In Stop 3, state that web engagement events are already stream-fed into Data Cloud.
  • In Stop 4, keep SMS scope focused on message build + flow inclusion.
  • Transition line: "Now that we know who gets what, let's show what they see."
  • Keep technical depth bounded to what changes send decisions.

Data Cloud Scope (Deliberately Narrow)

Show This

  • Einstein segment logic and output quality.
  • Calculated Insights used in qualification.
  • Engagement signals for suppression/exclusion.
  • Waterfall campaign prioritization method.

Avoid This

  • Ingestion and identity architecture detours.
  • Broad CDP replacement debates.
  • Execution-heavy process details not needed for demo value.
  • Live demonstration of dynamic feedback loop interactions in this session.
